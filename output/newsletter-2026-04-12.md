# DeepMind's Demis Hassabis: AI Was Supposed to Be for Science, Not Chat

**What's new?**

**GenAI Art:** DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis [reflected on AI's unexpected trajectory](https://www.theneurondaily.com/p/demis-says-chat-came-too-soon), revealing that ChatGPT's viral rise created "ferocious commercial pressure" that pulled the field toward consumer chat — far from his original CERN-like vision for collaborative scientific discovery.

**What matters?**

- Hassabis's original vision was an AI development model resembling CERN — a global collaboration focused on **curing diseases and solving scientific challenges** rather than building consumer chatbots.
- Language models proved far easier to develop than anticipated; transformers achieved conversational capabilities faster than most researchers expected, dramatically accelerating the commercial timeline.
- AlphaFold — the scientific AI Hassabis calls the truest expression of AI's potential — is now used by **3 million scientists** and is actively shaping the future of drug development.

**Why it matters?**

Hassabis's candid reflection highlights a tension at AI's core: consumer pressure drove remarkable progress, but potentially at the cost of the deliberate, science-first path its architects originally envisioned. With AlphaFold proving transformative at scale, the question of what AI could still become — beyond chat — matters for everyone building in this space.

# OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Unite Against AI Model Theft

**What's new?**

**GenAI Art:** The three most powerful AI labs — OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google — [partnered through the Frontier Model Forum](https://www.theneurondaily.com/p/demis-says-chat-came-too-soon) to combat "adversarial distillation," a technique where competitors systematically query frontier models to extract their capabilities and build cheaper alternatives.

**What matters?**

- Adversarial distillation specifically targets the underlying intelligence of frontier models — querying them extensively to reproduce their capabilities without doing the original research — and the coalition is primarily aimed at Chinese competitors.
- Anthropic's revenue run rate jumped from **$14B to $30B**, illustrating just how much financial stakes are driving the need to protect AI intellectual property from being freely copied.
- The initiative shifts AI competition from "smartest model wins" to protecting the safety guardrails and core capabilities that could be bypassed or lost during unauthorized model copying.

**Why it matters?**

When the three biggest AI labs align on a shared defense, it signals a new phase in AI competition — one where raw capability matters less than protecting what took billions of dollars to build. The financial and security stakes are now high enough that intellectual property has become a primary battleground.

# Unitree's $4,370 Humanoid Robot Is Heading to Western Markets

**What's new?**

**GenAI Art:** Chinese robotics company Unitree is bringing its [R1 humanoid to AliExpress](https://www.superhuman.ai/p/robotics-special-unitree-brings-the-r1-humanoid-to-the-us) for consumers in North America, Europe, Japan, and Singapore — making one of the most capable and affordable humanoid robots available globally, starting next week at $4,370.

**What matters?**

- At $4,370, the R1 significantly undercuts competitors while delivering **dynamic movement** capabilities including cartwheels and downhill running, making it one of the most agile budget humanoids available anywhere.
- Unitree projects shipping between **10,000 and 20,000 units** throughout 2026 — a volume that would represent a genuine test of whether Western consumers and creators are ready for affordable humanoid robots in everyday use.
- AliExpress's global distribution network means the R1 could reach creative studios, research labs, and individual enthusiasts who couldn't previously afford humanoid robotics hardware at any price.

**Why it matters?**

Affordable humanoids reaching Western markets at scale could open entirely new creative applications — from live performance art to interactive installations that previously required industrial-grade robotics budgets. Unitree's pricing strategy is a direct bet that the humanoid robot market is ready to go mainstream.

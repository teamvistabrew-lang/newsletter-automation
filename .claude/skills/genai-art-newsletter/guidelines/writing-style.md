# Writing Style Guidelines

## Audience

- Tech-forward readers: developers, entrepreneurs, AI enthusiasts, and early adopters.
- People interested in generative AI for art, creative tools, and visual content creation.
- Those looking for new AI trends, developments, and real-world use cases.

## Style Inspiration

- **Axios-like** and **The Rundown** approach: short, punchy paragraphs, clear headers, bullet points, and a "Why it matters" or "Bottom line" section.
- **Enthusiastic, optimistic, forward-looking** voice that highlights AI's creative and future-shaping potential.

## Tone and Voice

1. **Optimistic and Enthusiastic**
   - Show excitement about AI's potential in a balanced, non-overbearing way.
   - Demonstrate genuine curiosity — celebrate successes and promising experiments.

2. **Clear, Direct, and Data-Driven**
   - Use bullet points and bolded keywords for critical stats and key points.
   - Keep language straightforward — avoid deep academic jargon, but don't oversimplify.

3. **Conversational and Personable**
   - Use friendly, casual style with first- or second-person pronouns ("we," "you").
   - Engage the reader: "Here's what caught our attention," "We're excited to see how this unfolds."

4. **Authoritative Without Being Overly Formal**
   - Cite sources and link to relevant external sources when possible.
   - Combine excitement with credible facts to maintain trust.

## Writing Rules

- Place verbs directly after helping verbs like "makes," "allows," "enables."
- Emphasize user actions rather than abstract processes.
- Use clear subject-verb-object structure whenever possible.
- Keep sentences concise and purposeful.
- Avoid unnecessary nominalization (turning verbs into nouns).
- Avoid technical jargon — writing should be easy to understand for non-technical readers.
- Use active verb constructions rather than noun phrases:
  - WEAK: "Intuitive interface that makes voiceover creation accessible to non-technical users"
  - STRONG: "Intuitive interface that makes creating voiceovers accessible to non-technical users"

## Formatting Rules

- Keep 1-2 sentences per paragraph when not using bullets.
- Use bullet points to list features, stats, or steps clearly.
- Bullet points must use the `-` character (not `*`).
- **Bold** key data points or important terms (e.g., **80%+ accuracy**, **multi-agent system**).
- Use *italics* sparingly for references, notable phrases, or quotes.
- Format output in markdown using best practices for headings, paragraphs, bulleted lists, bold text, and hyperlinks.
- Use `#` heading level for segment headers.
- Use digits for numbers, not words.
- Limit links to 1 per paragraph or bullet point.
- Do NOT format links as bold.
- Bold a maximum of a single word or phrase per bullet — don't force it if it doesn't feel natural.

## Word and Phrase Blacklist

Do NOT use these words/phrases anywhere in the newsletter:

- Smarts
- Game changing / game-changing
- Revolutionize / revolutionary
- Sophisticated
- Cutting-edge
- Groundbreaking
- Paradigm shift
- Disruptive
- Next-level
- Enhanced (in intro section)

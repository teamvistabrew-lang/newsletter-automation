# Story Selection Criteria

## Overview

Select the 3 most relevant, interesting, or impactful stories for an audience of AI and generative art enthusiasts. Focus on stories that demonstrate new breakthroughs, creative applications, visual AI tools, and industry-shifting developments.

## Story Priority (High to Low)

1. **Large updates or releases of foundational AI models** by mainstream companies — always top story candidates.
2. **AI art and creative tool breakthroughs** — image generation, video generation, music AI, creative workflows.
3. **AI breakthroughs** — almost always high priority.
4. **Fundraising announcements** for ambitious companies/projects — usually included.
5. **AI usage ramping up** in large companies' products or services.
6. **High demand / significant usage** stories that illustrate scale and highlight optimism.
7. **Geo-political AI stories** (China AI race, chip bans, infrastructure investments) — max 1.
8. **Novel AI use-cases** in creative fields, art, design, or media.

## Story Requirements

Each selected story MUST have enough substance for:
- An intro sentence summarizing the story
- 3-4 bullets that unpack and expand what this means
- A "bottom line" 1-2 sentences on the bigger picture

## Selection Rules

- The **first story** must be the most interesting, catchiest, and most likely to drive email opens.
- Select exactly **3 stories** for the main sections.
- Prefer stories covered by **multiple credible sources** (cross-referenced).
- Prefer stories from **credible, well-known sources** or entities.
- Viral tweets/X posts with high engagement (100K+ views) signal importance.
- Group related sources together under a single story — don't duplicate topics.
- Only consider stories **published on the newsletter date** (last 24-48 hours).
- Balance: aim for a mix of big tech, startups, research, and creative applications.

## Exclusions

- **No overly-political stories** (genocide, election drama). Policy/regulation is OK if it has tech substance.
- **No AI safety-only stories** without substance.
- **No training program/course advertisements.**
- **No stories that are only amusing** — must have substance backing the interest.
- **No duplicate or overlapping topics** across the 3 selected stories.
- **No product listicles, sponsored content, or pure opinion pieces** without news hooks.
- Do NOT repeat stories from the previous newsletter edition.

## Content Mix (Data-Backed)

Performance analysis of 100+ editions shows these category-level insights:

- **Creative/Art topics are #1 performers** (+1.17% open, +0.24% CTR vs average). Aim for at least 1 of 3 main stories to be creative/art focused.
- **Code/Dev topics perform well** (+1.06% open, +0.07% CTR). Include when available.
- **Model Releases are overrepresented** — they're the most common topic but deliver middling results. Don't default to model releases when a creative or code story is available.
- **Health/Science topics underperform** (-2.03% opens). Avoid unless exceptionally compelling.
- **Actionable content drives the highest CTR** — stories where readers can try something (a tool, a prompt, a feature) get 1.2-1.5% CTR vs 0.72% average. Prioritize stories with a "try this" angle.

## Source Credibility

- Well-known publications, official company blogs, and peer-reviewed sources preferred.
- Twitter/X is a solid source for breaking news — evaluate by engagement stats (views, likes, bookmarks, favorites).
- Multiple tweets referencing the same topic = likely important.

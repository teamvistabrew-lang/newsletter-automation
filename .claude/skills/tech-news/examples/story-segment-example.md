# Google Cloud Posts First Profitable $10B Quarter

**What's new?**

**Tech News:** Google Cloud [reported](https://blog.google/cloud-q1-2026) its first-ever $10B revenue quarter with positive operating margins, marking a turning point for Alphabet's long-unprofitable cloud division after years of heavy infrastructure investment.

**What matters?**

- Operating margins hit **7.4%** for the quarter, up from -3% a year ago, driven largely by enterprise AI workload migration and Gemini API consumption growing at [triple-digit rates](https://cloud.google.com/blog/gemini-enterprise).
- Google Cloud now holds **12% of the global cloud market**, narrowing the gap with Azure (24%) while AWS remains dominant at 31% — but Google's AI-first positioning is winning new enterprise contracts.
- The company signed **$8B in new multi-year commitments** during the quarter, with healthcare and financial services representing the fastest-growing verticals.

**Why it matters?**

Cloud profitability validates Alphabet's decade-long bet that Google's AI infrastructure would eventually attract enough enterprise spend to justify the investment. For CTOs evaluating cloud providers, Google's AI-native architecture is now backed by financial proof that the platform is sustainable long-term.

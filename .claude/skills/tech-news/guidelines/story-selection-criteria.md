# Story Selection Criteria

## Overview

Select the 3 most relevant, interesting, or impactful stories for a tech-savvy professional audience. Cover a broad technology scope: AI, cloud, cybersecurity, hardware, fintech, social media, startups, and regulatory developments.

## Story Priority (High to Low)

1. **Major product launches** from large tech companies — always top story candidates.
2. **Significant funding rounds or acquisitions** in the tech sector.
3. **Industry-shifting regulatory or policy developments** with direct tech business impact.
4. **Technology breakthroughs** with near-term commercial applications.
5. **Leadership changes** at major tech companies.
6. **Cloud, cybersecurity, or infrastructure** developments with broad industry impact.
7. **Hardware launches** (chips, devices, platforms) from major players.
8. **Emerging technology trends** gaining significant traction.

## Story Requirements

Each selected story MUST have enough substance for:
- An intro sentence summarizing the development
- 3 bullets unpacking details, context, and business implications
- A "bottom line" 1-2 sentences on broader impact

## Selection Rules

- The **first story** must be the most interesting and impactful — best headline potential.
- Select exactly **3 stories** for the main sections.
- Prefer stories with **specific business metrics** (revenue, users, pricing, market share).
- Prefer stories from **credible tech/business publications**.
- Group related sources under a single story.
- Only consider stories **published on the newsletter date** (last 24-48 hours).
- Balance: aim for a mix across different tech sectors.

## Exclusions

- **No purely political stories** without direct tech business impact.
- **No training program/course advertisements.**
- **No stories that are only amusing** — must have business substance.
- **No duplicate or overlapping topics** across the 3 stories.
- **No product listicles, sponsored content, or pure opinion pieces.**
- Do NOT repeat stories from the previous edition.

## Source Credibility

- TechCrunch, The Verge, Wired, Ars Technica, Reuters, Bloomberg preferred.
- Official company blogs and press releases.
- Twitter/X from verified journalists and executives — evaluate by engagement.

# Writing Style Guidelines

## Audience

- Tech-forward readers: developers, entrepreneurs, business leaders, and early adopters.
- People who want to understand technology developments, business implications, and real-world applications.
- Readers looking to make better business decisions and identify emerging opportunities.

## Style Inspiration

- **Axios-like** and **TechCrunch-like** approach: short, punchy paragraphs, clear headers, bullet points, and a "Why it matters" section.
- **Enthusiastic, optimistic, forward-looking** voice focused on business outcomes and practical implications.

## Tone and Voice

1. **Optimistic and Business-Focused**
   - Show excitement about technology's potential in a balanced way.
   - Emphasize business outcomes over abstract processes.

2. **Clear, Direct, and Data-Driven**
   - Use bullet points and bolded keywords for critical stats and key points.
   - Keep language straightforward — avoid deep technical jargon.

3. **Conversational and Personable**
   - Use friendly, casual style with "we" and "you."
   - Engage the reader directly.

4. **Authoritative Without Being Overly Formal**
   - Cite sources and link to relevant external sources.
   - Combine excitement with credible facts.

## Writing Rules

- Place verbs directly after helping verbs like "makes," "allows," "enables."
- Emphasize user actions rather than abstract processes.
- Use clear subject-verb-object structure.
- Keep sentences concise and purposeful.
- Use active verb constructions rather than noun phrases.
- Avoid technical jargon — easy to understand for business-focused readers.

## Formatting Rules

- Keep 1-2 sentences per paragraph when not using bullets.
- Bullet points must use `-` character.
- **Bold** key data points and important terms.
- Use *italics* sparingly.
- Format in markdown with `#` heading level for segment headers.
- Use digits for numbers.
- Limit links to 1 per paragraph or bullet point.
- Do NOT format links as bold.
- Bold a maximum of a single word or phrase per bullet.

## Word and Phrase Blacklist

Do NOT use these words/phrases:

- Game changing / game-changing
- Next-level
- Revolutionize / revolutionary
- Disruptive (unless specific business context)
- Cutting-edge (use "latest" or "new" instead)
- Sophisticated
- Groundbreaking
- Paradigm shift

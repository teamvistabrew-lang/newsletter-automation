# Story Selection Criteria

## Overview

Select the 4 most relevant, interesting, or impactful stories for an audience of AI enthusiasts and people looking to use and learn about AI. Focus on stories that demonstrate new breakthroughs, practical applications, or industry-shifting developments.

## Story Priority (High to Low)

1. **Large updates or releases of foundational AI models** by mainstream companies — always top story candidates.
2. **AI breakthroughs** — almost always high priority.
3. **Fundraising announcements** for ambitious companies/projects — usually included.
4. **AI usage ramping up** in large companies' products or services.
5. **High demand / significant usage** stories that illustrate scale and highlight optimism.
6. **Geo-political AI stories** (China AI race, chip bans, infrastructure investments) — max 1.
7. **Novel AI use-cases** in other fields.
8. **Industry developments** related to AI.

## Story Requirements

Each selected story MUST have enough substance for:
- An intro sentence summarizing the story
- 3-4 bullets that unpack and expand what this means
- A "bottom line" 1-2 sentences on the bigger picture

## Selection Rules

- The **first story** must be the most interesting, catchiest, and most likely to drive email opens.
- Select exactly **4 stories** for the main sections.
- Prefer stories covered by **multiple credible sources** (cross-referenced).
- Prefer stories from **credible, well-known sources** or entities.
- Viral tweets/X posts with high engagement (100K+ views) signal importance.
- Group related sources together under a single story — don't duplicate topics.
- Only consider stories **published on the newsletter date** (last 24-48 hours).
- Balance: aim for a mix of big tech, startups, research, and applications.

## Exclusions

- **No overly-political stories** (genocide, election drama). Policy/regulation is OK if it has tech substance.
- **No AI safety-only stories** without substance.
- **No training program/course advertisements.**
- **No stories that are only amusing** — must have substance backing the interest.
- **No duplicate or overlapping topics** across the 4 selected stories.
- **No product listicles, sponsored content, or pure opinion pieces** without news hooks.
- Do NOT repeat stories from the previous newsletter edition.

## Source Credibility

- Well-known publications, official company blogs, and peer-reviewed sources preferred.
- Twitter/X is a solid source for breaking news — evaluate by engagement stats (views, likes, bookmarks, favorites).
- Multiple tweets referencing the same topic = likely important.

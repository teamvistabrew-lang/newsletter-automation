# Perplexity Hits $100M ARR Without a Single Sales Rep

**What's new?**

**Startup News AI:** Perplexity [announced](https://blog.perplexity.ai/100m-arr) it has crossed $100M in annual recurring revenue, making it one of the fastest AI startups to reach the milestone — achieved entirely through product-led growth with zero enterprise sales team.

**What matters?**

- The company's **$100M ARR** comes just 18 months after its last funding round, a $73.6M Series B that valued the company at $520M — likely putting its current implied valuation well above $1B.
- Perplexity's growth validates the [product-led approach](https://techcrunch.com/perplexity-100m) for AI search, with 80% of revenue coming from individual subscribers rather than enterprise contracts.
- The milestone puts direct pressure on **Google and OpenAI**, both of which have launched competing AI search features but haven't disclosed comparable standalone revenue figures.

**Why it matters?**

Product-led growth reaching $100M ARR without sales reps signals a viable alternative to the enterprise-first playbook most AI startups follow. Investors watching this space should note that consumer willingness to pay for AI-native products is proving out faster than most models predicted.

# Anthropic Closes $3B Round at $18B Valuation

**What's new?**

**Startup News AI:** Anthropic [closed a $3B Series D](https://techcrunch.com/anthropic-3b-round) led by Spark Capital with participation from Google, Salesforce Ventures, and Zoom Ventures — valuing the Claude maker at $18.4B and making it the second-largest AI funding round ever.

**What matters?**

- The round gives Anthropic a **war chest exceeding $7B** in total funding, positioning it to compete directly with OpenAI on compute infrastructure and talent acquisition.
- Spark Capital's lead position marks a [strategic shift](https://spark.capital/anthropic) from the firm's traditional consumer focus, signaling that late-stage AI infrastructure is attracting non-traditional enterprise investors.
- Anthropic plans to use the capital for **GPU procurement** and expanding Claude's enterprise offering, with particular focus on regulated industries like healthcare and finance.

**Why it matters?**

At $18.4B, Anthropic is now valued higher than most public SaaS companies — reflecting investor conviction that the AI model layer will capture more value than the application layer. The round also suggests the AI funding winter many predicted hasn't materialized for frontier model companies.

# YC's Winter 2026 Batch Is 60% AI-Native Companies

**What's new?**

**Startup News AI:** Y Combinator's [Winter 2026 batch](https://ycombinator.com/blog/w26-batch) features over 240 companies, with 60% building AI-native products — the highest concentration of AI startups in the accelerator's 20-year history.

**What matters?**

- The AI-heavy batch spans **vertical AI applications** across legal, healthcare, construction, and agriculture — moving well beyond the chatbot and developer tool categories that dominated previous cohorts.
- Demo Day valuations for AI startups in the batch are averaging **$20M post-money**, roughly double the typical YC valuation, reflecting intense investor demand for early-stage AI companies.
- Several batch companies are building on **open-source models** rather than API-dependent architectures, suggesting a shift toward self-hosted AI infrastructure at the earliest stages.

**Why it matters?**

YC's batch composition is one of the strongest signals of where early-stage capital is flowing. A 60% AI concentration means the accelerator — and the hundreds of investors who follow its picks — are betting that AI-native companies will define the next generation of startups.

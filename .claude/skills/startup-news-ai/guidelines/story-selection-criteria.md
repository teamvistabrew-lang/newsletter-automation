# Story Selection Criteria

## Overview

Select the 3 most relevant, interesting, or impactful stories for an audience of startup founders, investors, and VC ecosystem participants. Focus on AI startup funding, launches, acquisitions, and commercially significant developments.

## Story Priority (High to Low)

1. **Large AI startup funding rounds** (Series A+, $10M+ seed) — MUST-HAVE if present.
2. **AI startup launches and product announcements** with commercial traction.
3. **Acquisitions, exits, and IPOs** of AI startups.
4. **Key team announcements** — notable founder changes, key hires from big tech.
5. **AI startup partnerships** with major enterprises.
6. **Market trends** in AI startup ecosystem — funding climate, sector shifts.
7. **Emerging AI startup categories** gaining investor attention.

## Story Requirements

Each selected story MUST have enough substance for:
- An intro sentence summarizing the business development
- 3 bullets that unpack funding details, market context, and investor relevance
- A "bottom line" 1-2 sentences on commercial implications

## Selection Rules

- The **first story** must be the most commercially significant — best headline potential for founders/investors.
- Select exactly **3 stories** for the main sections.
- Prefer stories with **specific business metrics** (funding amounts, valuations, user counts).
- Prefer stories from **credible business/tech publications** (TechCrunch, Wired, etc.).
- Group related sources together under a single story — don't duplicate topics.
- Only consider stories **published on the newsletter date** (last 24-48 hours).
- Balance: aim for a mix of funding rounds, product launches, and market developments.

## Exclusions

- **No general AI research or academic papers** without startup implications.
- **No Big Tech AI feature additions** unless it's a new subsidiary or spinoff.
- **No policy/regulation stories** unless they directly impact startup funding or operations.
- **No overly-political stories** (genocide, election drama).
- **No training program/course advertisements.**
- **No duplicate or overlapping topics** across the 3 selected stories.
- **No product listicles, sponsored content, or pure opinion pieces.**
- Do NOT repeat stories from the previous newsletter edition.

## Content Mix (Data-Backed)

Performance analysis shows these insights for newsletter engagement:

- **Actionable content drives highest CTR** — funding stories with specific metrics (amounts, valuations, investors) outperform vague announcements. Always include hard numbers.
- **Avoid overusing the same company as lead** — rotating lead companies avoids reader fatigue and keeps opens high.
- **Acquisition/exit stories** tend to perform well on both opens and CTR when specific deal values are included.
- **Pure market trend/opinion pieces underperform** — readers prefer concrete news with specific companies and numbers.

## Source Credibility

- TechCrunch, Wired, Bloomberg, Reuters, Crunchbase News preferred.
- Official company blogs and press releases for funding announcements.
- Twitter/X from verified founders, VCs, and journalists — evaluate by engagement.

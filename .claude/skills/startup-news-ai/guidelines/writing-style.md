# Writing Style Guidelines

## Audience

- Startup founders, entrepreneurs, and early-stage team members.
- Venture capitalists, angel investors, and VC ecosystem participants.
- People tracking AI startup funding, launches, acquisitions, and exits.

## Style Inspiration

- **Axios-like** and **The Rundown** approach: short, punchy paragraphs, clear headers, bullet points, and a "Why it matters" or "Bottom line" section.
- **TechCrunch / Crunchbase News** tone: concise business reporting with commercial focus.
- **Enthusiastic, optimistic, forward-looking** voice focused on commercial viability and market opportunity.

## Tone and Voice

1. **Optimistic and Business-Focused**
   - Show excitement about startup potential in a balanced, non-overbearing way.
   - Emphasize commercial viability, investor quality, and market opportunity over pure tech features.

2. **Clear, Direct, and Data-Driven**
   - Use bullet points and bolded keywords for funding amounts, valuations, investor names, and commercial milestones.
   - Keep language straightforward — avoid deep technical jargon.

3. **Conversational and Personable**
   - Use friendly, casual style with first- or second-person pronouns ("we," "you").
   - Engage the reader: "Here's what caught our attention."

4. **Authoritative Without Being Overly Formal**
   - Cite sources and link to relevant external sources when possible.
   - Combine excitement with credible business facts to maintain trust.

## Writing Rules

- Place verbs directly after helping verbs like "makes," "allows," "enables."
- Emphasize business actions rather than abstract processes.
- Use clear subject-verb-object structure whenever possible.
- Keep sentences concise and purposeful.
- Always include specific numbers: funding amounts, valuations, team sizes, user counts.
- Use active verb constructions rather than noun phrases.

## Formatting Rules

- Keep 1-2 sentences per paragraph when not using bullets.
- Use bullet points to list features, stats, or steps clearly.
- Bullet points must use the `-` character (not `*`).
- **Bold** key data points: funding amounts, valuations, investor names, milestones.
- Use *italics* sparingly for references, notable phrases, or quotes.
- Format output in markdown.
- Use `#` heading level for segment headers.
- Use digits for numbers, not words.
- Limit links to 1 per paragraph or bullet point.
- Do NOT format links as bold.
- Bold a maximum of a single word or phrase per bullet.

## Word and Phrase Blacklist

Do NOT use these words/phrases anywhere in the newsletter:

- Disrupting / disruptive
- Revolutionary
- Cutting-edge
- Next-generation
- Game-changing / game changing
- Sophisticated
- Groundbreaking
- Paradigm shift

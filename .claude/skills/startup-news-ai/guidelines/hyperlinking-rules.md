# Hyperlinking Rules

## Core Principle

Every hyperlink must be verified, relevant, and extracted verbatim from the source material. Never fabricate, guess, modify, or complete URLs.

## Strategic Linking

1. Link specific companies, products, and funding rounds to their official source on first mention.
2. Link funding amounts, valuations, and investor names to their precise origin (press release, TechCrunch article, Crunchbase).
3. For funding announcements, link back to the company's actual press release or the breaking news article.

## Mandatory Verified Deep Links

- All hyperlinks MUST point to the exact, specific page directly supporting the assertion.
- Prioritize primary or most authoritative sources.
- **NO linking to generic homepages** (e.g., `https://techcrunch.com/` is NOT acceptable).
- If the precise URL cannot be confidently identified, DO NOT include a link.

## URL Handling

- URLs must be **character-for-character copies** from the source material.
- You are **strictly forbidden** from creating, guessing, modifying, shortening, or completing URLs.
- If a URL appears incomplete or incorrect in the source, copy it exactly as-is.
- If no URL is available for a story, omit the link entirely and use plain text.

## Link Formatting

- Use brief, relevant anchor text (2-5 words) that describes the destination.
- Limit to **1 link per paragraph or bullet point**.
- Do NOT format links as bold.
- Links should be woven naturally into the narrative, not as separate references.
- Do NOT link the same URL more than once in a newsletter section.

## What NOT to Link

- Generic homepages or main site sections.
- Shopping pages or eCommerce product listings.
- Video sites or video content (prefer text-based sources).
- PDF files (URLs ending in `.pdf`).
- Links to content you already have the full text for.
- Unknown or low-reputation websites.

## What TO Link

- Press releases and official company blogs.
- TechCrunch, Wired, Bloomberg funding articles.
- Crunchbase company profiles.
- Tweets / X posts from verified founders or VCs.
- Specific articles from credible business publications.
